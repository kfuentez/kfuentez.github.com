Scenarios

Just like any situation, you don't know how you're going to act until you're in it. There are several incidents where a bad experience may accidentally happen, so we want to help you see these incidents and know how it can be prevented.

Unwanted Pressure

You and your boyfriend have been dating a few months now. He's a great guy and your family loves him. But lately, he's been pressuring you to have sex without a condom. You both agree that it feels better, but you aren't on the pill, and you don't want to risk an unplanned pregnancy. You've told him you prefer using condoms but he insists on going without. What do you do?

A) Dump him. He should respect your body and choices.
B) Just keep having sex the way he wants. He pulls out, and you don't want to fight.
C) Firmly tell him you won't have sex unless he uses a condom. You still want to be with him but you refuse to have unsafe sex.
​

Sure, maybe it's not worth the fight. Sometimes it's just best to agree to disagree, and you can find someone else who holds the same views as you.
Pulling out doesn't always work. Pre-cum does contain some sperm, and it's not even guaranteed that he always pulls out in time.
Stand your ground. You know that safe sex is important, and if he wants this relationship to work, he will compromise with you.

Get Checked

One day, you notice a small bump on your vagina. You start to panic, but you know you've used a condom every time, and all the guys you were with swore they were clean. You're from a really small town so if you go to any clinic, everyone will find out.

B) Just brush it off. It's probably an inanswer-grown hair.
C) Call the last guy you slept with and ask if he has any STD.
​

Great idea. Condoms are not 100% guaranteed to protect from STDs, and just because a guy says he is clean, doesn't mean he is. You shouldn't be embarrassed by STD testing; it's a regular, healthy thing that every sexually active woman should do for her own safety.
